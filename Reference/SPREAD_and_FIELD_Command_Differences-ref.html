SPREAD and FIELD Command Differences

The two field/energy distribution commands, SPREAD and FIELD, appear to perform similar calculations (and in fact can perform many of the same calculations), but there are differences described in this topic.

By default, SPREAD and FIELD calculate the energy or peak flux density relative to the local propagation direction at each sample point. Use the IRRADIANCE X, Y, or Z prior to SPREAD or FIELD to calculate the irradiance relative to the given fixed direction for all sample points.

  • Slicing the Irradiance Pattern with FIELD
    FIELD does not have a DOWN option like the SPREAD command, but it is possible to obtain the same information by selecting a narrow WINDOW width in the down direction.
  • Slicing the Irradiance Pattern with SPREAD
    If you do not need to calculate the entire 3D energy distribution, such as when symmetry is expected, it is more expedient and less time consuming to calculate only a slice of the irradiance pattern.
