Optimization - Glossary

This topic provides a glossary of terms for the optimization feature in ASAP.


Term

Definition

Design Objective Real-valued property of a system under evaluation (SUE) fully determined by the values of the design variables
Design VariableReal-valued degree of freedom of a SUE; variables are essential in optimization; an ASAP script (INR) file that contains optimization elements such as a scalar real variable must be open in the Editor window to perform an optimization
Design Variable LimitBound on the range of a design variable
FeasibilityCategorical metric of the "suitability" of a trial solution
Figure of Merit (FOM)An overall measure of the optimality of a design as measured by the weighted sum of squares of the deviations of the design objectives from their respective target values
Manufacturing ToleranceQuantitative measure of the meaningful precision of a design variable, typically chosen to be proportional to the manufacturing process variability or minimum "tool-step" size
Objective TargetDesired or "target" value for a design objective
Objective ConstraintA direct or indirect relationship among the design objectives used to quantify feasibility; constraints are not essential
OptimizationIn ASAP, optimization of a design is achieved by seeking a minimum of the FOM as a function of the design variables and optionally subject to objective constraints.
Penalty FunctionCan be used to guide constrained optimization; penalty functions can be defined from an ASAP script and then assigned to constraints
Penalized Figure of Merit (PFOM) Sum of the FOM and the values of the Penalty Functions associated with Objective Constraints which are both enabled and violated
SUESystem Under Evaluation, described by a combination of fixed parameters and one or more design variables
Trial SolutionConfiguration of the SUE evaluated for a specified set of design variables
