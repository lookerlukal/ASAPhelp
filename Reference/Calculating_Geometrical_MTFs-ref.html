Calculating Geometrical MTFs

In optical systems where the residual aberrations are large, the diffraction MTF can be difficult to compute. Consequently, an analogous measure of performance, the MTF is defined. Whereas, the diffraction Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) is the Fourier transform of the Point Spread Function (PSF), the geometrical MTF is a Fourier transform of the spot diagram.

The geometrical MTF is calculated in ASAP in the same manner as the diffraction MTF. The major difference is that the SPOTS command is used rather than the SPREAD or FIELD commands to generate the PSF. Polychromatic calculations are done by incoherently summing several monochromatic PSFs and then Fourier transforming.

The WINDOW and PIXEL commands should be set as per the instructions in Calculating MTFs. The spatial frequency cutoff is the hard upper limit on resolution regardless of whether the calculation is performed in the geometrical or diffraction regime.

A note on geometrical MTFs: a geometrically perfect system produces a PSF that is essentially a delta function. From Fourier transform theory the transform of the delta function is unity at all spatial frequencies. This is clearly not physical because in those instances diffraction determines the cutoff frequency. It is therefore important to consider the validity of geometrical MTFs of near ideal systems.
