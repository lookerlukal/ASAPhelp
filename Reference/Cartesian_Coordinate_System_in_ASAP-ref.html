Cartesian Coordinate System in ASAP

ASAP uses a single, global, right-handed, Cartesian coordinate system with components x, y, and z. "Edges", "lenses", and "surfaces" are the basic geometrical entities of the program.

Edges (or curves) are made up of a series of connected points in space.

Lenses are arbitrary sequences of circular conicoids.

Surfaces are represented mathematically by a single equation f(x,y,z)=0 and are either infinite in extent or self-enclosed (for example,. a plane or sphere, respectively). The volumes of space between surfaces can be assigned refractive properties and are called "media".

Objects are lenses, several surfaces or many connected curves bounded by other surfaces and/or edges, and represents something that can absorb, refract, reflect, diffract, polarize, or scatter radiation.

Tip

Unless otherwise stated, ALL COORDINATES ARE GLOBAL.
