Edges and Curves Overview

Edges and curves are made up of a series of connected points in space that form a parametric representation of an entity.


  • The total number of points that can be connected together depends on whether you have ASAP lite or another ASAP configuration. (Edge and curve are equivalent key words. The distinction is provided to give you the ability to distinguish between commands used as geometric and mathematical curves.)
  • Unless otherwise stated, all coordinates are global.
  • The first point defined on the edges/curves (using POINTS or LINE) or the vertex point on the coordinate axis (using ELLIPSE, RECTANGLE, ROUNDED and others) is called the reference point. The reference point has the following properties:
  • Edges/curves may be defined relative to this point.
  • Edges/curves may be located in space with this point. For most edges with initial axial symmetry, this point is the intersection of a coordinate axis with the edge.
  • Simple translations/shifts of edges/curves are performed on the reference point.
  • ASAP stores the name of the command used to generate the edges/curves, but does not store either the arguments of the command or the individual transformation commands.

  • Since entering even simple edges/curves directly via points would be tedious, ASAP includes several predefined edge/curve command types:

    ARC

    BEZIER

    CHARACTER

    CONIC

    ELLIPSE

    HELIX

    IMAGE

    LINE

    OVAL

    POINTS

    RACETRACK

    RECTANGLE

    ROUNDED

    SAWTOOTH

    SPLINE, EXPLICIT 2D

    SPLINE GENERAL 3D

    USERCURV

Tip

All edges/curves are autolimiting by definition.
