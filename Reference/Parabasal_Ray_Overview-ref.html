Parabasal Ray Overview

This topic describes two types of parabasal rays.

Not all beam shapes (apodizations) can be exactly modeled with real rays. However, J. Arnaud discovered that Gaussian beams may be accurately modeled by real rays. These rays are in a sense paraxial because they describe the local field about a base ray. The base ray is the conventional ray that is always traced, such as a ray defined by a GRID command. The rays around the base ray, describing the field properties of the beam, are called parabasal rays.

The two types of parabasal rays are:


Waist Ray:Defines the beam semidiameter near or at a waist
Divergence Ray:Defines the beam asymptotic divergence of the beam away from the waist
