  • Geometric Entities
  • Ghost Image Analysis
    Ghost images are produced by inter-reflections of light from optical element surfaces that have nonzero reflection and transmission coefficients. Some of the incident light is transmitted at a surface of the element while some is reflected at the same surface. The reflected light propagates back to another element surface, is reflected there, and eventually propagates to the image plane or detector. The culmination of these reflections results in a ghost image.
  • Glass Catalogs for Refractive Media
    The glass databases accessible in ASAP include: French, Hikari, Hoya, Hoya New, Ohara, Schott, Schott New, Sumita, and Unusual. The catalogs are in text files named *.CAT. The data for a specific glass consists of the glass name and six interpolation constants. No transmission, absorption, cost, staining data are contained in this database.

  • Lens Entity
    ASAP defines a lens entity to be a sequence of refractive or reflective conicoid surfaces that are bounded by circular apertures and separated by homogeneous MEDIA.
  • Lens Modifiers
    Unlike surfaces and edges that have numerous modifiers, only two types of modifiers are supported for lens entities: linear transformations and alteration of specific lens construction information (using the ALTER (Lens Modifier) (ASAP Command).
  • Linear Transformations for Edges and Curves
    Linear transformations change the shape, scaling, or orientation of a geometrical entity (surface, edge, lens, object, group, or rays) by applying a general 4x4 transformation matrix (linear transformations change only the orientation of lenses). Linear transformations that change the location of a geometrical entity are applied to the reference point (see table) of the entity.
