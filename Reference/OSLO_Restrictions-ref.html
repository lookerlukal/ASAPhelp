OSLO Restrictions

This topic describes specific features and limitations of the OSLO to ASAP translator.

While the OSLO to ASAP translator supports most of the optical system modeling capabilities of OSLO, there are inevitable differences in the database structure and intended applications so that a precise translation is not always possible.


Alternate surface intersectionNot supported.
Apertures Only simple circular apertures and one and two special apertures are supported. In the latter case, the apertures are assumed to be circular, regardless of the specific OSLO selection. Other aperture types such as those specified with vertex coordinates and aperture rotations are not supported.
Aspheric surfaces

While OSLO is capable of modeling a very high order surface ( > 20th-order in most cases), ASAP is currently limited to 20th-order. Coefficients of higher order are written to the ASAP file, but not modeled.

AS0 term in the ASR aspheric surface is not supported.

Radius of curvature in the ISR aspheric surface must not be zero.

ISO x and y-toric surfaces (TRX and TRY) are not supported.

Only even polynomial terms in x and y are supported in the BIC biconic and CNX asymmetric cone surfaces.

Only rotationally symmetric Zernike terms to 6th-order are supported.

Boundary drawing Not supported.
Diffractive surfacesWhile OSLO is capable of modeling a very high order diffractive surface (>20th-order in most cases), ASAP is currently limited to 20th-order. Coefficients of higher order are written to the ASAP file, but not modeled.
Signs of HV1 and HV2 Must be set manually.
DF0 coefficient in the DFR and DRA diffractive surfacesNot supported.
DXA asymmetric CGH (absolute value) diffractive surfaceNot supported.
Zernike phase surfaces ZRR and ZRX Not supported.
Eikonal surfacesNot supported.
Extruded surfaces Not supported.
Gradient index (GRIN) media

In evaluating the refractive indices during a raytrace through a gradient material, OSLO is more forgiving than is ASAP. If OSLO encounters a situation where the refractive index is complex or cannot be evaluated, OSLO sets the refractive index for that step to n0 (the base index) and continues. ASAP, on the other hand, aborts with an error message.

Only the reference wavelength gradient function is written to the ASAP model.

Only terms up to z³ are supported in the GRD gradient. Furthermore the dispersion model is not supported.

Only terms up to z² and ² are supported in SNS gradient.

Only terms up to z² and ρ4 are supported in TAP gradient.

User-defined gradients are not supported.

Lens arraysNot supported.
Operating conditions Operating conditions such as WARM, TELE, AFO, and so on, do not affect the system geometry, therefore they are not translated. You need to construct the desired raytrace characteristics.
Perfect lensIf the magnification of a perfect lens is set to unity, the translator writes it out as an infinite conjugate due to a mathematical degeneration.
PickupsNot supported. The translator requires a complete specification of a lens system.
Skip surfaceNot supported.
SolvesNot supported. The translator requires a complete specification of a lens system.
Surface drawing controlsNot supported.
Surface groupsNot supported.
Thermal expansion, atmospheric and pressure data Not supported.
User-defined surfacesNot supported.
WavelengthsThe current version of ASAP supports 15 wavelengths.
Zoom (multiconfiguration) systemsOnly the base configuration is translated.
