Simulating Diffractive and Holographic Optical Elements

Diffractive Optical Elements (DOEs) are a general class of optical elements that include diffraction gratings, holographic optical elements (HOEs) and binary optics. These elements diffract light by producing periodic changes in the phase or amplitude of an incident wave. This topic addresses DOEs that change the phase of incident optical fields.

Two common approaches to calculating the diffraction pattern from DOEs are:


  • Calculating the directions of propagation of the various reflected and transmitted orders of a beam after interacting with the DOE as well as the fractional power contained in each order using vector diffraction theory. This complicated calculation involves not only the DOE structural data, but also the physical and electromagnetic properties of the DOE. ASAP does not perform this type of approach.
  • Calculating the idealized directions of propagation of the various reflected and transmitted orders of a beam after interacting with a diffractive element using scalar diffraction theory. The diffraction orders are selected by adjusting the direction cosines of the exiting beams according to the grating equation. The diffraction efficiencies cannot be calculated with this method since this is a scalar technique. Most optical design and analysis software, including ASAP, use this technique since it is very adaptable to ray tracing.

ASAP models DOEs by simulating the phase surface of the DOE as a series of grating lines. Replicating the grating line spacing mathematically simulates the grating lines. The replication is then associated with the base entity of an object. After intersecting the base object, ASAP computes the local grating spacing and applies the grating equation to compute the direction of propagation. Moreover, since ASAP can split rays, it can also propagate several diffracted orders simultaneously after the intersection.

Tip
  • To model the phase surface of a DOE as a series of grating lines, you must use another modifier to the surface database called MULTIPLE, which creates multiple sheets of a surface to simulate the grating lines. Also use the object modifier, INTERFACE, to assign a diffractive interface to an object.
  • The phase surfaces of an optical field exiting a DOE may exhibit 2- step discontinuities. Although the wavefront shows the phase steps, SPREAD and FIELD removes the discontinuities in the process of synthesizing the optical field.
