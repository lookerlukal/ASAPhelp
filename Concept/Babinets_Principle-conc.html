Babinet Principle

Babinet’s principle is for modeling difficult diffraction problems. Set the amplitudes of certain beams to be of opposite sign from other beams, so beam amplitudes are in effect subtracted from one another.

In the Example of Babinet’s Principle, thin spider vanes and a central obscuration are modeled with sources whose amplitudes are -1, and so their contributions to the total optical field are subtracted rather than added. This is more efficient than increasing the ray density in hopes that the vanes are sufficiently sampled. DECOMPOSE POSITION can also be used to perform this calculation.
