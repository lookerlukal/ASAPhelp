Abel Transform Overview

The Abel transform and its inverse are used in image reconstruction problems to recover a 3D distribution from its projections; that is, the tomography problem.

The Abel transform is preferred when the distribution has circular symmetry, as in the case of an arc source. The Abel transform, which is a special case of the Radon transform, also requires that the distribution exhibit little or no self-absorption.

Reference
 The Fourier Transform and Its Applications, R. N. Bracewell, 2nd ed. 1986, McGraw Hill pp 262-266.
