Bending Factor

A zero bending factor is a common parameterization in optical engineering. A zero bending factor implies that the lens is symmetric; both sides of the lens will have the same radius of curvature in opposing directions. Other bending factors allow the generation of unbalanced, plano concave, or meniscus lenses. All having the specified focal length, but differ in the aberrations produced.
