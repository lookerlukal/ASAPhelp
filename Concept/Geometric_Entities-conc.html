Geometric Entities

Edges, lenses, and surfaces are the basic geometrical entities of ASAP:


  • Edges are made up of a series of points in space.
  • Lenses are sequences of circular conicoids.
  • Surfaces are represented mathematically by a single equation f(x,y,z)=0 and are either infinite in extent or self-enclosed (that is, a plane or sphere, respectively).

An object is one surface or lens, or several edges bounded by other surfaces and/or edges, and represents something that can absorb, refract, reflect, diffract, polarize, or scatter radiation.
