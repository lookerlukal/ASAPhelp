ASAP Import from IGES - Accuracy

The accuracy of the ASAP import capability cannot be generally specified. The accuracy is dependent upon a number of parameters and must be treated on a case-by-case basis. This topic addresses some accuracy issues.

When ASAP imports an IGES entity, it simulates the entity as a series of 2nd-order, piecewise Bezier meshes. The meshes are adjusted to have G1 continuity. This means that the actual piecewise meshes are forced to be continuous in first derivative (the meshes are continuous in form but not necessarily curvature). Although this continuity condition is not as stringent as G2 continuity where the meshes are continuous in first and second derivatives, it is more stringent than C1 continuity where the meshes are parametrically continuous up to first derivative. The continuity cannot be increased unless order of the piecewise Bezier meshes is increased beyond 2nd order.

Individual IGES knot sequences are translated into either one or two 2nd-order Bezier meshes. Two Bezier meshes are used if the curvature of the IGES entity is large. Simulating higher-order curves or surfaces with 2nd-order Beziers can be a problem in certain situations. For example, a 5th-order NURB (non-uniform rational B-spline) defined over a single knot sequence is not accurately modeled by one- or two-piecewise, continuous 2nd-order Bezier meshes. In this situation, you must increase the number of knot sequences in your CAD system to increase the number of 2nd-order piecewise G1 continuous Beziers that are used to simulate that portion of the surface. Increasing the number of knot sequences leads to longer ray trace times, since you are effectively increasing the number of objects in the ASAP model. However, this is currently the only methodology available to improve the accuracy of the translation if the accuracy is in doubt.
