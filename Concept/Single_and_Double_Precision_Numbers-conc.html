Single- and Double-Precision Numbers

Single-Precision (SP) uses 32-bit IEEE floating point numbers with seven decimal digits of accuracy. Double-Precision (DP) uses 64-bit IEEE floating point numbers with 15 decimal digits of accuracy. In the large number of floating point operations needed to trace a ray, rounding error will make the accuracy in the final results much less. For example, OPL (optical path length) accuracy is on the order of five decimal digits for SP and 11 for DP.
