Polychromatic Diffraction MTF of a Doublet System

The calculation of the polychromatic Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) involves performing an Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) on the Point Spread Function (PSF). The Example of Poly PSF and MTF of a Doublet has an MTF analysis done at 0.5 degrees off-axis.

  • Polychromatic Diffraction PTF of a Doublet
    The Example of a Polychromatic PTF of a Doublet illustrates both MTF and PTF calculations superimposed on the same plot for easy comparison of the individual curves (MTFs are normalized to 1, PTFs are not normalized). The optical system under analysis is an achromatic doublet.
  • Polychromatic Geometrical MTF of a Doublet
    The example shows a geometrical Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) calculation of a doublet. Since it is very well corrected, the geometrical MTF is a bit anomalous at the higher spatial frequencies. For comparison, two focal positions are analyzed: nominal focus (optimized over the entire 1-degree full field of view) and best axial focus.
