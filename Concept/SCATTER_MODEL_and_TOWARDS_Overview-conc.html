SCATTER MODEL and TOWARDS Overview

This topic gives several tips for scattering rays towards a specified small area.

Tips
  • Useful for performing stray light analysis.
  • The specified area toward which rays are scattered is defined with one or more elliptical or rectangular edges. Scattering into a cone that is centered on the specular direction or the surface normal is also possible.
  • SCATTER MODEL and TOWARDS follow an OBJECT command.
  • A model and any important edges must be defined before using these commands (there are no forward references).
  • ASAP scales the flux of the scattered ray, taking into account the flux of the incident ray, solid angle of the important edge, BRDF model, angles of incidence and scatter, and cosine obliquity factors of the surface and important edges.
  • To obtain correct results in most cases, the diameter of the important edge must be small compared to the distance to the important edge.
  • The TOWARDS SPEC command, with a small cone angle and appropriate BRDF model, may be used to simulate variations in reflectivity with angle of incidence. Make the object ABSORBing and scatter only a single ray.
