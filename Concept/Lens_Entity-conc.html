Lens Entity

ASAP defines a lens entity to be a sequence of refractive or reflective conicoid surfaces that are bounded by circular apertures and separated by homogeneous MEDIA.

When assigned as a whole to an OBJECT, rays from other objects can intersect only the first or last conicoid of the lens. The ray then propagates sequentially through the lens until it fails or exits at one of the ends.

The advantage of a LENS object over individual SURFACE (for example, OPTICAL) objects is not only simplicity but also speed in both raytracing and 3D graphical representation (see PLOT LENSES).
