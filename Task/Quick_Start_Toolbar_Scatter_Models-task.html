Quick Start Toolbar - Scatter

The Scatter button on the Quick Start toolbar opens the Scatter page—a list of available scatter and roughness models that you can apply to objects.

Other:

Diffuse black surfaces are typically Lambertian at normal incidence, but their BRDF increases dramatically in the forward scatter direction at high angles of incidence. This model of a diffuse black assumes a diffuse reflectance of 5% at normal incidence. The BRDF is much larger than 1 in the forward scattered direction when the angle of incidence is high.

Diffuse white surfaces, such as paper, flat white paints, and materials used for integrating spheres, typically maintain their Lambertian character over a wide range of incident angles. This model of a diffuse white surface assumes a diffuse reflectance of 97%, and is Lambertian for all angles of incidence. Note that Lambertian character of diffuse white surfaces will fail at very high angles of incidence.

Polished Mirror (visible wavelengths). The BRDF of polished mirrors almost always follows a Harvey scatter model. The parameters of the model used here are typical of good quality, clean, polished mirrors at visible wavelengths.

Polished Mirror (infrared wavelengths). The BRDF of polished mirrors almost always follows a Harvey scatter model. The parameters of the model used here are typical of good quality, clean, polished mirrors at infrared wavelengths. Be aware that even a small amount of dust (particulate contamination) on a mirror’s surface will greatly increase its BRDF at infrared wavelengths. Therefore, this model is valid only for pristine surfaces.

E60L is a microvoided, white film with low specific gravity and high reflectivity, and is designed as a reflector plate for LCD backlighting. (Lumirror E60L is a registered trademark of Toray Industries, Inc.)

POC LSD Diffuser Sheets. To apply Diffuser Sheet models to objects, you must right-click a model and select Apply Models. You can drag and drop a diffuser sheet onto the Builder. However, unlike other models in ASAP, diffuser sheets cannot be dragged and dropped onto the ASAP Object Tree.

Diffuse Metal. Scatter from diffuse metal surfaces often follows a three-parameter Harvey model. The total integrated scatter of the model used here is 75% at normal incidence. The angular width (half-width) of the scattered light is approximately 10 degrees.

Vacuum Metalized Reflector. A Harvey model is used to describe the scattered light from a vacuum metalized reflector. The model used here assumes that most of the light from the surface is reflected, as the total integrated scatter of this model is only 0.25%.

Etched Plastic. The Harvey model that is used here is for transmissive diffusers. The BTDF of diffusers varies widely from one diffuser to another, so this model should only be used for rough calculations in the early stages of a design. The width of the scatter distribution is about 8 degrees (half width), and the total integrated scatter is 90%.

Alanod, MoldTec, Tenebac. Data from selected texture samples from these manufacturers is included.

Notes on Scatter Models


  • With the exception of the Diffuser models, you can drag and drop a scatter model onto an object (or parent) in the ASAP Workspace window after creating a system. This applies the selected scatter model using a TOWARDS SPEC scattering into the full hemisphere.
  • When a model is applied via the ASAP Workspace window, the Command Output window shows the commands that ASAP is using to apply the scatter model to the selected object(s).
  • All scatter models can be dragged and dropped onto Editor or Builder windows. In this case, only the MODEL is inserted at the cursor location. Users need to apply an object using the SCATTER MODEL; TOWARDS commands.
  • The Command Output window shows the commands that ASAP is using to apply the scatter model to the selected object(s).
  • If the scatter model is dropped on an object that has an interface coating, a message notifies you that an interface coating will interfere with scatter model properties. You may choose to continue or cancel the operation.
  • If you try to apply a model, and the maximum number of models has been reached, you are notified and the model will not be applied. See the reference topic, ASAP Configurations for information on the maximum models supported.
  • The roughness models that are available from the Quick Start toolbar are basic. They do not implement the TOWARDS command; therefore, ASAP assumes that the rays will scatter in a hemisphere.
  • If the roughness model is dropped on an object that does not have an interface coating, a message notifies you that a lack of an interface coating will interfere with roughness model properties. You may choose to continue or cancel the operation.
