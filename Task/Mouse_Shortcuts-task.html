3D Viewer Mouse Shortcuts
Mouse Shortcuts

The 3D Viewer supports the mouse shortcuts described in this topic.


Rotating about screen axesClick the right mouse button and move the mouse to rotate the view. Horizontal (vertical) mouse movement corresponds to rotation about the vertical (horizontal) screen axis.
Rotating with one axis locked verticalHold down the Control and Shift keys and click the right mouse button. The 3D Viewer 'locks' one axis to the vertical screen plane (yz plane in screen coordinates). Horizontal mouse movement rotates about this locked axis, while vertical mouse movement rotates about the horizontal screen axis.
ShiftingHold down the Shift key and click the right mouse button while moving the mouse to shift the geometry within the view.
Scaling by mouse movementHold down the Control key and click the right mouse button while moving the mouse to scale the view. Upward movement zooms inward (magnifies), while downward movement zooms outward (reduces) the view. Horizontal movement has no effect.
Scaling by mouse wheelRoll the mouse wheel away from you (up) to magnify the view, or roll the wheel towards you (down) to reduce the view.
Selecting objectsClick the left mouse button to select the object under the cursor.
Zooming on an objectDouble-click the left mouse button to select an object and zoom the view to its extents. If no object is selected, the view is zoomed to the extents of all currently visible objects. As with all zoom extents operations, the center of the extents becomes the new rotation center.
Shifting slice planes in volume visualizationsSlice planes for volume visualization objects can be moved with the mouse. Hold down the Shift key and click the left mouse button while the pointer over the desired plane. The slice plane will follow mouse movements.
